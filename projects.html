Sago Po ​ Bubble Tea
Ideation

Sago Po is a hypothetical, family-run, Filipino-inspired bubble tea shop located in the bustling heart of Midtown Toronto. Sago Po's mission is to fill everyone's cups — metaphorically-speaking too!

The intent was to make Filipino flavours appealing to urban teenagers and/or young adults (especially Filipinos but not strictly so) in a way that doesn't dilute Filipino culture. Initially, I knew I wanted the brand logo to incorporate a Filipino elderly (since "po" is a signifier of respect in sentences and verbal conversations). But I also wanted it to be playful.

Variations and Iterations
Learning Hurdles

• Balancing complexity in form, colouring and shading with minimalism so that the logo design is scalable for smaller sizes (ie. stickers, favicons)

Learning Outcomes

• Repurposing brand assets and visuals for different mediums

• Text hierarchy

• Demonstrating non-destructive editing techniques in Photoshop

Windsong Festival
Ideation

Windsong is a hypothetical opera festival held in Taipei, Taiwan, and follows the tales of forbidden lovers who love is cradled, carried and delivered by the wind.

The intent was to blend Chinese/Taiwanese aesthetics with contemporary minimalism to create cultural/folk, modern/elegant visuals. To fulfill the cultural/folk visuals, I initially planned to incorporate national colours (ie. some combination of red, magenta, gold, blue, jade), recurring nature elements (ie. water ripple/waves, wind lines, the moon) and recurring symbols (ie. the plum blossom and traditional Chinese patterns). To fulfill the modern/elegant visuals, I initially planned to pair gold with red and/or blue, and pair a script font with a serif or sans-serif font, using thin lines will reinforce this further.

Sketches
Learning Hurdles

• Working with a colour palette with more than 3 colours, and mixing up their roles (ie. primary, secondary and accent colours)

• Having colours within the larger colour palette create colour dissonance depending on how they're used together

Learning Outcomes

• Interpeting a professional design brief

• Repurposing brand assets and visuals for different mediums

• Text hierarchy

• Script type should be used sparingly as it can harm both legibility and readability. This is especially the case for script type with thin, natural strokes.
